A Comprehensive Guide On Proton Therapy For Pancreatic Cancer

Here is everything you need to know about proton
beam therapy for pancreatic cancer, its benefits,
efficacy, and survival rates, and more.

MAKE AN APPOINTMENT

As pancreatic cancer becomes increasingly common, much research and money are invested into finding effective treatment options.
Treatment regimens may differ depending on cancer's nature, extent and location. Some of the prominent pancreatic cancer types include
exocrine pancreatic cancer (Adenocarcinoma, Squamous Cell Carcinoma, Adenosquamous Carcinoma, Colloid Carcinoma),Neuroendocrine
pancreatic cancer, and Benign Precancerous lesions.

Imaging tests, such as CT, PET and MRI scans, Endoscopic Ultrasound (EUS), biopsy and blood tests to gauge the occurrence of tumour
-marker proteins, are often recommended for diagnosing pancreatic cancers. In most cases, surgery is the first step of the treatment. However,
occasionally, if the surgery can't be performed or proves inadequate to contain cancer, proton therapy and chemotherapy are used.

Benefits of Proton Theraphy for
Pancreatic Cancer

Proton treatment for pancreatic cancer is highly effective and commonly used in pre-operative
and postoperative settings. Using proton therapy reduces exposure to radiotherapy-related
toxicity. Consequently, it can offer improved results by allowing an increased radiation dose and/
or chemotherapy intensification.

How Effective Is Proton Therapy In
Treating Pancreatic Cancer

The efficacy of proton therapy for pancreatic cancer is higher when combined with
chemotherapy. In a 2019 study, it was seen that 56% of candidates who underwent proton
therapy survived three years longer.

Further, it was noted that candidates treated with proton therapy for pancreatic tumours
were much less likely to experience severe side effects compared to traditional radiation therapy.
While it improved the quality of life, there was no distinct difference in their survival rate.

Pancreatic Cancer Statistics
And Risk Factors

While pancreatic cancer has a relatively low incidence in India, it ranks 18th in mortality, making
it a deadly disease. Risk factors are divided into the following categories.

Modifiable Risk Factors

  • Smoking
  • Increased alcohol consumption
  • Obesity
  • Sedentary lifestyle
  • Chronic pancreatitis

Non-Modifiable Risk Factors

  • Age - Increased incidence with advancing age, especially between 65 to 75 years
  • Gender - Males are twice as likely to develop pancreatic carcinomas, predominantly due to increased tobacco usage
  • Family History -Genetic predisposition as pancreatic cancer can be hereditary among some families

Pancreatic Cancer Statistics
And Risk Factors

One of the best ways to prevent pancreatic cancer is by reducing risks from modifiable factors.
Stop smoking, exercise regularly to maintain a healthy weight and have a healthy lifestyle with a
balanced diet.

Treatment options include surgery, chemotherapy, radiation therapy and photon therapy for
pancreatic cancer.

How Can Patients Prepare For Proton Therapy Treatment
For Pancreatic Cancer?

Before patients undergo proton radiation therapy for pancreatic cancer, there is a radiation
simulation and imaging test to ensure that the proton beam hits the right spot. Patients may
undergo magnetic resonance imaging (MRI), or computerised tomography (CT) scans for
imaging and path determination before the treatment.

Which Pancreatic Cancer Patients Benefit From
Proton Therapy?

Many patients opt for proton therapy for stage 4 pancreatic cancer when the cancerous growth
>is widespread and advanced. In such cases, the size or position of the tumour makes it
inoperable. Hence, using proton therapy, patients can receive localised treatment to destroy and
prevent the regrowth of cancerous cells. Moreover, it is preferred among older patients who may
show an increased risk of radiation toxicity or be unable to handle chemotherapy.

What Can The Patients Expect From Proton Therapy
For Pancreatic Cancer?

Proton therapy delivers a beam of proton particles directed at the tumour to stop growth. Since it
uses a target delivery system, it prevents any damage to surrounding healthy tissues.
Additionally, patients undergoing this treatment will likely see more effective results with fewer
side effects than traditional radiation therapy and chemotherapy, which can cause toxicity.

In most cases, surgery is the first in the line of treatment. It is followed by chemotherapy and/or
proton therapy for pancreatic cancer for optimal results, especially if the cancer is advanced.

How much time is required for proton therapy treatment
in pancreatic cancer?

Depending on the nature of the cancer and the set treatment regimen, photon therapy sessions
are held three to five days per week for an extended duration. During this, progress is noted, and
changes are made to the treatment plan if required.

Typically, proton beam therapy for pancreatic cancer lasts only a few minutes. However,
treatment preparation may take around 30 to 45 minutes before the session, making the process
longer.
